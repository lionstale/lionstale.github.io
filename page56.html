Reading Suggestions for the Spring and Summer Season

By: Ilinca Maria-Manda

“A reader lives a thousand lives before he dies.

The man who never reads lives only one.”

As the weather gets warm and the sun that we have seen so little of starts to shine again, we all start to feel like going out more in order to make the most of it. However, the pleasant spring-like atmosphere when the trees are in bloom and nature comes back to life is also a perfect opportunity to savor a good book! We can now comfortably read outside in the garden, while enjoying a good seasonal treat. This is a beautiful scenery, and the right book will make everything perfect!

Below are some suggestions from the category “warm weather reads” of all genres which are ideal to read anywhere, anytime (during spring or summer) … Is there anything better than good weather and a good book to go with it?

The Great Divorce
by C.S Lewis

A short but great philosophical story, for anyone who has ever wondered about the myths of the afterlife.

The White Queen
by Philippa Gregory

If you fancy history and are intrigued by the stories of the great female leaders of whom we may have heard in history classes, Philippa Gregory does a great job telling us more about them. In The White Queen, we are given a great insight into the life of Elizabeth Woodville and her great destiny as Queen of England.

The Constant Princess
by Philippa Gregory

The amazing story of Catherine of Aragon, the forgotten wife of King Henry the Eighth, in which Gregory masterfully depicts Catherine’s struggles, as well as her true strength: beauty and courage. Better than many movies!

A Doll’s House
by Henrik Ibsen

For all feminists and theater fans out there, this will be a delight! Although it was considered outrageous in its time, this play is catchy, intense, comic and full of unexpected “miracles”. It’s a lot of fun to read, as well as to see on stage!

Emma
by Jane Austen

Coming from the best 19th Century British author, this is the story of a woman who lives a privileged life and who has everything she needs… but does she really? The main charm of Emma is that she usually is quite relatable, but never fails to remain a great, funny character (as well as an awful match maker)!

Sense and Sensibility
by Jane Austen

Another amazing read from the same classical author, this book is about a journey, both physical and emotional, in the romantic realm of 19th century England. It takes us through a multitude of emotions and a few great life lessons, given in the Austen style!

Love Her Wild
by Atticus

Although a recent apparition, Love Her Wild is an enchanting journey for anyone drawn to words, romance, and poetry. It can easily become the kind of book that you will always want to have near!

Milk and Honey
by Rupi Kaur

If you enjoy poetry of simple verse but with strong meaning, Milk and Honey is a beautiful read with very touching content, which takes us through the process of “the hurting” to the “the healing” of any heart.
A read suitable for spring!

All the Light We Cannot See
by Anthony Doerr

Set in France with the backdrop of the Second World War, this book is rich in imagery and metaphors, as well as in emotion. It will surely leave its mark!

Wuthering Heights
by Emily Bronte

A swift return to the 19th century classics, Wuthering Heights is a love story unlike any other. Whether despised or adored, this book knows how to leave its mark through its plot, characters, and gothic setting.

Paris is Always a Good Idea
by Nicolas Barreau

A light read for a beautiful spring or summer day, this book has nothing but good vibes and carries quite a bit of the Parisian atmosphere. Perfect for hopeless romantics like the main character, as well as for anyone who agrees with the title. Paris surely is a good idea!

The Girl Before
by J.P. Delaney

If you enjoyed “Gone girl” or “The Girl on the Train”, this book is surely the one you are looking for. It will keep you awake all night, but you will not regret it, as the story is unique and full of masterfully created mystery.

The Truth About the
Harry Quebert Affair
by Joel Dicker

A twisted and complex psychological thriller, this book is unlike any other of its kind. If plot twists and suspense are something you enjoy, this book has plenty of both, as well as a story that
will keep you captivated until you reach the end.

The Book Thief
by Markus Zusak

The touching and intense story of Liesel, a young girl who develops a love for reading to escape the hard reality of war, as well as the poverty she must endure in her small German town. The book beautifully presents the struggle between innocence and maturity, as well as the immense inner strength required to get through the loss of loved ones during the Nazi regime. The book also has a great film adaptation!
