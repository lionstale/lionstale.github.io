Reading Suggestions for the Cold Season

By: Ilinca Maria Manda

Got the winter blues? Or on the contrary, are you excited for Christmas and holidays, warm treats and fuzzy clothes? Either way here is a way to make it all better, as a good book is never a wrong idea when it is too cold outside and you just want to chill by the fire with a cup of hot chocolate in your hand. 

Here are a few books absolutely perfect for this time of the year! Each one of them can carry you to a different place, from love stories to adventures in faraway lands, philosophy, crime, mystery and even some history! Whatever your preference is, take a bit of time and consult the list below when in the mood for a good story or just a beautiful escape! As they say, a bit of dreaming never does any harm, especially if done in the company of the right book.

A quick overview of some great reads…

‘Pride and Prejudice’ by Jane Austen

A classical 19th century love story from one of the best British Authors, this book is ideal for hopeless romantics and for those with a passion for the Victorian Era. The story is unforgettable, and be not mistaken; it is not the usual, cheesy kind of love story, but something far more special. Also a good read if you would like to learn some 19th century British pick-up lines!

‘And then there were none’ by Agatha Christie

A small, but intense and twisted story, which will not be able to put down or forget too soon! If you enjoy crime and mystery, this is the one for you! Ideal to read before going to sleep (so that you don’t even want go to sleep.)

‘Sophie’s world’ by Jostein Gaarder

Any interest in the history of philosophy and in the mysteries of human existence? If you wish for a good and complete introduction in this field, then this book will do perfectly. It responds in a simple manner to a wide range of complicated issues and is really thought provoking! And remember: all that I know is that I know nothing!

‘The Light between Oceans’ by M.L. Stedman

A heart-breaking and uncommon love story which takes place on a remote island found between two great oceans. Remember to always keep the light going! It is an intense and absorbing read.

‘Cartes Postales from Greece’ by Victoria Hislop

Nostalgic for the summer holidays and warm, sunny countries? This book will take you on an amazing tour around the whole of Greece and give you an insight in to the life there! And on top of that, a beautiful storyline to follow!
Note: Comes with some beautiful images as well, hence a great travel guide! 

‘Eat Pray Love’ by Elizabeth Gilbert

A woman’s search for internal peace in three countries, through a beautifully crafted memoir! A calming and highly entertaining read for everyone!

‘The Art of Hearing Heartbeats’ by Jan-Philipp Sendkerr

An inspirational and timeless love story set in Burma, between the 1950’s up to the present. It will leave you breathless and with tears in your eyes! To be read while sitting by the fire on a cold autumn/winter night.

‘The Nightingale’ by Kristin Hannah

The story of two French sisters during the Second World War, this book is about love, bravery and war. Much better than many movies and very inspirational, as it emphasizes the strength of the bond between sisters, which is strongest in hard times.
To read if you're looking for an intense experience of the Second World War! 

‘Beautiful Broken Things’ by Sara Barnard

How much can a life-long friendship between two girls endure a third person entering the game? A realistic and interesting tale of friendship and teenage struggles.

‘All the Bright Places’ by Jennifer Niven

A poetic romance between two teenagers with sad backgrounds. Worth the wait!

‘Gone With the Wind’ by Margaret Mitchell

Set with the Backdrop of the American Civil War, ‘Gone with the Wind’ will take you to faraway lands and back in time! It presents the story of a young woman whose life changes radically throughout the war and has to do her very best to survive this change.

‘The Kite Runner’ by Khaled Hosseini

A powerful story about Afghanistan and its people. It will move you and change your perspectives. Also a story about redemption, the ‘Kite Runner’ is full of authenticity and feeling.

‘The Beautiful and Damned’ by F. Scott Fitzgerald

If you enjoyed the ‘Great Gatsby’, this is another masterpiece of the same author, with quite a few important life lessons and unique characters!

‘Before I Fall’ by Lauren Oliver

Everything is connected in this world and our actions can affect other people’s lives more than we know. Written mainly for teenagers, ‘Before I fall’ is an incredible and insightful journey which can truly open our eyes about what it means to live our lives well.

‘The Paris Winter’ by Imogen Robertson

An enchanting story from “La Belle Époque,” following the life of an artist in Paris that strives to earn a living, but unexpectedly ends up trapped in a world full of dark secrets and twisted stories.

‘The World’s Wife’ by Carol Ann Duffy

Fond of poetry? Here are some great works with unique messages and intense verse, coming from the perspectives of women we encountered in history, music, fairy-tales and literature! Impossible to not love!
