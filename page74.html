IB Visual Art Winter
Show and MHS Art and
Design Showcase

A beautiful collection of art work by a great number of St. John's students.

Article By: Aimee Dastin-van Rijn

The Winter Art Show was a great success with parents, teachers, and students coming to appreciate the wide variety of art created by high school students. For those who did not attend, or those who did and did not get the chance to speak to many artists, below are explanations of a few artists’ work and their inspirations.


Many students focussed on global issues through their art, as well as challenging people’s perspectives on ideas.


Madeleine Bitter:

Madeleine found a way to connect two of her interests, art and geography, to create pieces which involve world issues. She focused on global mass consumption and the environment, hoping that through art she could show people that these are problems which need society’s attention.

Allegra Nicoletti:

Allegra focussed on countering the expectations many have about photography, words, and language. 


Through her first piece, she challenged set ideas about photography. Instead of standard rectangular pictures, she created a piece which forced the pictures to adapt to a circular shape. She used many different images and connected their lines, so from far away they look as if they are all one image, but it is truly a combination of many. The jagged lines of the image help to enhance this idea of non-conventional photography.


In her second piece, she challenged the way we see words and language, by making words into a sculpture and entrapping them in glass.


Her third piece combined the two ideas to show the explicit connection between words and photography. Using poems she makes from books in combination with photos, Allegra superimposed the two worlds to highlight a relationship between the literary and artistic realms that many would not have considered.

Anna Hilton:

The main idea of Anna’s work was human destruction of the planet and it expressed her frustration and anger towards how ignorant we can be. The pieces show how Anna sees the issue, using the idea of burning as an unexpected metaphor. As burning is not often used in artistic pieces, it helped to surprise the viewer and make them consider the ideas the piece proposed. The series of images is a criticism to society, showing the world before, during, and after humanity’s influence on it.

The idea of the environment was continued through other students’ pieces which focused on nature. 


Leslie Romo:

Leslie’s pieces focused on investigating the theme of leaves and trees. In the various colors and designs, she perfectly portrayed the beauty in Autumn which had captured her eye.

Eliska Varilova:

Eliska told a story her grandfather used to tell her as a child of when he found a bird and kept it as a pet. He told her how the bird used to sit on the piano keys when he would play and ride on the handlebars when he rode his bike. The way she portrayed it in the pictures is the way she sees it in her mind.


Her second piece was a painting which has many ideas behind it, one of the main ones being that your ideal self is holding you true self back.

Other students continued on from this idea of representing through that which cannot always be seen with the naked eye. 


Elina Ly:

Elina created two self portraits, the first portraying the thoughts in her head. It is not only a self-portrait of her physical appearance, but her thoughts and emotions, too, expressed through words and drawings. In combination with her second portrait, this piece helps to show the divergence between what you can see and what you cannot.


Through the portrait separated into three sections and the loose design of the background, Elina’s piece ensured that the focus was drawn to the center of the face.

Ashley Webster:

Ashley’s pieces are beautiful representations of music through art. Ashley incorporates elements of synaesthesia into her art, inspired by the artist Melissa S. McCracken. Each one of Ashley’s pieces is a representation of how a song she listened to made her feel. Some other pieces are also inspired by the galaxy and the Milky Way.

Marie Wuyts:

Marie created a fashion line modeled by various students. She describes her pieces and the meaning behind them in the statement below:

Models walking the runway in the middle of an art exhibition holding posters stating, “Social Media Seriously Blinds You.” Ironic? Yes. Everything that happened during Friday’s event was recorded with the purpose of posting it on social media later on. It was nonetheless appropriate for such an event where students exhibit their pieces of art so that everyone can look at it, as in the modern day, people tend to look at not much other than their cell phones, and it is a shame.
While paintings can be appreciated on a wall, I believe that clothes lose their value if they are not worn. Further than that, my theme for this collection was “deconstructed reconstructed.” I combined very different fabrics in each dress which made them interesting in terms of movement. I recycled used clothes into my designs, with all the jeans pieces coming from jean jackets found in vintage stores, and the rest from various clothes. I tore them apart and reconstructed them into something completely different. The “Juliette” dress had the most interesting process as half of it was made out of 10 cm squares which were cut out of several different jean jackets and then reassembled into a mosaic-like dress. 
I could explain each piece in details for hours, but instead I will highlight that was I not only proud to be able to show my designs, but I also had an amazing times interacting with my “models” and creating a show with them.
