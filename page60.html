IB Senior Art Show

Our IB Art seniors presented their last show on April 20th
and auctioned some of their work for the Rahula Trust.

On Friday, April 20th, the IB seniors had their final art show, which also happens to be the final exhibition in the Greene Gallery. As observers walked through the rooms lit by the uncharacteristically bright Belgian sun shining through the skylights, they could see the personality of each artist displayed through their work. Teachers, parents, and students alike marveled at the amount of creativity present in one class.


Many of the artists began their journeys during the identity project in junior year. Josephine, whose work elicited many high bids during the auction, said that she tried to explore identity through art, “which led her to questions like ‘What is art? Is this art? And What is realistic?” Any person who had the pleasure to look at her work on Friday would definitely have agreed that it was art. Another artist, Lara, also explored identity through different perspectives on layers of the human body which were very intriguing.

That last picture features another artist, Sara, who explained that, “the process is very long”, and the piece you see is, “the result of experimentation that you wouldn’t see in the final.” She found that it is important to, “take time to enjoy the art, not just for a grade, but for you.”


After the attendees had taken time to admire the work, it was time for the auction to begin upstairs. Mr. Mitchell began with a speech focusing on the journey his students have taken as IB Art students. He discussed their place in the modern globalized world and that many would go on to study art, but others will not (hopefully continuing art as a hobby). He quoted Socrates who said, “I am not an Athenian or a Greek, but a citizen of the world.” Our artists are becoming citizens of the world who will hopefully go on to inspire others to do the same through education. As John, the auctioneer, presented the works of art for sale, 11th grade members of the Greene Gallery team retrieved the art from the bottom floor to display. Everyone present enjoyed John’s jokes and cheery demeanor throughout the whole auction. Overall, the auction managed to raise 1440 euros for the Rahula Trust, which sponsors education for the world’s poorest children.
