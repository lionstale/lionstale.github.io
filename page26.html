Time to Grow Up
Mr. Kim Jong-Un

Written By: Eva Morris

Three weeks ago, on August 29th, at 6:02AM, just four minutes after North Korea’s launch of a ballistic missile over Northern Japan, millions of Japanese citizens woke abruptly to an emergency text message reading, “Missile launch. Missile launch. A missile was fired from North Korea. Please evacuate to a sturdy building or basement.” 


Ai Onodera, a Japanese citizen who was fast asleep in Hokkaido's capital, Sapparo, said, “I was terrified that I would not see [my husband] again.” Although North Korea’s missile landed in the sea after almost reaching 1,700 miles, innocent Japanese families were left distressed and helpless. A wave of shock and apprehension reverberated around the world. Kim Jong-un, the grinning, eccentric, North Korean leader and the youngest head of State in the world, had proved again he is probably one of the most dangerous rulers in current history. 

Since then, there have been two more test missiles directed by Kim Jong-un. After close to four hours in an emergency session in response to the missile attack, the United Nations Security Council strongly condemned North Korea’s actions, and stated that it was “not just a threat to the region but all U.N. member states.” The United Nations recommended last year that Kim Jong-un and his leadership be brought before the International Criminal Court in The Hague. Japan’s Prime Minister Shinzo Abe said that “North Korea’s reckless action of launching a missile that passed over Japan is an unprecedented, serious and grave threat.” There is no doubt that Kim's message extends way beyond the Japanese waters and could be seen as a very direct warning towards the USA. President Trump is doing all in his power to ensure North Korea is unable to develop nuclear missiles that can hit the mainland United States. However, one cannot, of course, ignore Trump’s retaliatory tweets about being “locked and loaded,” and wonder whether a more diplomatic approach is more prudent.  


It seems to be that some people in life start a course of action with complete disregard to what is right and what is wrong, whether it is a school bully or the notorious Kim Jong-un, who appears to have a complete disregard to international laws and indifference to just about every other country in the world. It is as though Kim Jong-un has been allowed to indulge his own whims, with no-one daring to challenge his actions nor address his anger management problems. He appears indulged and has thrown his toys out of his pram. However, in this case, he has thrown out a ballistic missile out of his pram.

"It seems to be that some people in life start a course of action with complete disregard to what is right and what is wrong"

Despite increasing security and international laws, we are all living in very unpredictable times. Kim Jong-un’s cult following and grinning stance still does not deter from the fact that he is running a nuclear missile programme and currently holds over 120,000 political prisoners in North Korea. It is like a time-bomb waiting to go off. No-one really knows much about Kim Jong-un, however we do know, as a dictator, that he rules by divine right and has a strange taste in haircuts. So for the sake of our security, and the safety of future generations, perhaps it is time to grow up Mr. Jong-un.
