Applying to the UK

Written By: Dominic Gray

In theory, applying to the UK is very straightforward. All you need to do is fill out the UCAS form with your personal details, write one personal statement of up to 4000 characters (including spaces) and decide on your 5 choices. 


With regard to your choices, you can apply to up to 5 choices. These choices can be the same subject at 5 different universities, or different subjects at the same university, or even a combination of the two. It is important to think carefully about your choices, since you will end up narrowing them down to two: your firm choice (the one you most want to go to) and your insurance choice. You will do this once you have received a response from all of your choices (either an offer or a rejection.) Typically, you want the insurance choice to have a lower number of points required so that you have a sort of safety net. You should make sure, however, that you would still be happy going to your insurance choice if you werenâ€™t fortunate enough to meet your top offer. 


Something that needs to be taken into consideration when deciding your choices is that you only get one personal statement that will be sent to all 5 choices. If at one university you apply for a single honours course (one subject) and at the same or another university you apply for a joint honours course (two subjects), you have to be able to write a personal statement that satisfies both courses, which can be tricky. I would suggest, therefore, applying to different universities all for the same (or very similar) course, so it simplifies the process of writing the personal statement. 

"It is often recommended that the personal statement is 80% academic and 20% extra curricular."

It is often recommended that the personal statement is 80% academic and 20% extra curricular. Therefore, you should focus on the aspects of your education that will lend itself to why you will be successful studying the course you have chosen. With regard to the extra curricular actives that you mention in your PS, I suggest using them as a means to show the qualities that you have which can be applicable to studying a demanding course. For example, I mentioned how playing musical instruments for many years demonstrates my motivation and my dedication which are qualities necessary to be successful for a university course. 


This process will be for you towards the end of 11th grade, when you can make an account on UCAS and start drafting your personal statement. Remember, however, it is never too early to start considering universities and courses. Since you have to pick only 5, it is better to start earlier and consider all of the options so you have enough time to narrow down to the choices you are happy with rather than rushing at the last minute and making the wrong decision.


Good luck with your application!
