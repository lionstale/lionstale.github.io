Lucky to be Lions

Written By: Ceola Daly

It’s a new year at St. Johns and as we all prepare our school books and schedules, I’m reminded of how proud most of us are to rejoin our sports teams; whether it be out there in the courts while we’re playing against ISB or cheering from the side of the field during our away games, all of us are proud to be Lions. But how important is that title? The fact that we call ourselves Lions, and not Tigers or Jaguars like a few of the other schools that participate in the ISSTs. The purpose of a mascot is to make the school community united in the name of a character that represents their spirit and energy. For some schools, these mascots are quite obvious; for example, our school is situated near the battle of the Waterloo, an event commemorated by the Lion’s Mound completed in 1826. But there are some school mascots that perhaps are not as straightforward…  

Picture this: you’re watching St John’s in the final of the Varsity Boys Soccer Team, and just as they’re about to score the winning goal the entire St John’s stand starts cheering “Go Hot Dogs!” at the top of their lungs. The boys miss the shot. This is a very possible occurrence at Frankfort High School in Indiana, US, whose mascots are ‘The Hot Dogs.’

If that scenario doesn’t sound too strange to you, then try this one: You’re watching the Middle School Girls’ Basketball Team at the end of the second quarter, and just as the teams go off for a huddle a Junior walks onto the court dressed head-to-toe as a polka dot, a seemingly hilarious instance available to the Poca High School Poca Dots of West Virginia.


All of these situations probably sound laughable, but it highlights the reason that many schools even have school mascots – no matter how involved in the school you are, it will still represent even a small part of your life. How big that part is, is up to you. So get into your Lions gear and be ready to cheer for the Lions for another year! Go Lions!
