My Icelandic Boyfriend

Written By: Aimee Dastin-van Rijn

This summer, on our trip to Iceland, my parents brought along a family friend who I'm pretty sure they were trying to set me up with (classic PVR.) His name was Jeffrey, and at the beginning of the trip, I had no idea how close we would become or how much we would do together. On the plane ride over, Jeffrey was closed off from the world and unenthusiastic, despite me trying to cheer him up. During the car ride to Reykjavik, he started to open up and was more fun to be around. He was finally warming up to me and I was pretty happy about it. He wasn't necessarily the traditional kind of hot, but on a scale of hotness, he was definitely getting up there. 

That night, we walked the streets of Reykjavik together and gazed romantically over the city skyline. We ate dinner and even went for froyo. That night, we slept in the same room, but not in the same bed (don't worry, we had only met that morning.) The next morning, we went to Reykjavik's Penis Museum together (not really my idea of a perfect date, but fun either way.)

Throughout the trip, we took many long, romantic walks, ate all our meals together, and cuddled in the car. One night we went to a sushi restaurant, which is the perfect date. He was a complete gentleman and refused to let me split the bill. We also did the touristy things, including seeing far too many Icelandic waterfalls.

During our trip, he got really jealous of my Icelandic friend Skyr. I met Skyr the same day I met Jeffrey and we became obsessed with each other. Jeffrey couldn’t see that it was just a close friendship with no romance. Skyr and I didn't spend all our time together because he would get moody. It's like he has a flavor for every day of the week, although mostly just sweet. Jeffrey didn't see it that way though, and we argued about it constantly. 

 At the end of the trip, I had to say bye to both Skyr and Jeffrey. At the airport I just couldn’t bring myself to leave Jeffrey, so I got him a last minute ticket to America with me. I left behind my friend Skyr, but I’m sure we'll meet again in the future, because a friendship that sweet can’t end that way.
