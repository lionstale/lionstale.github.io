Painting the
Fugitive Image

Time, Memory, and Imagination

Artist: Alan Mitchell

Article Written By: Aimee Dastin-van Rijn

The most recent St. John’s Art Show was an exhibition of Alan Mitchell’s work. Inspired by civilizations before us and the painters of the Renaissance Period, his work explores illusions and the transitory nature of many aspects of life. He uses a variety of mediums from oil paint to mixed media on paper to find a, “melancholy beauty and poetry” in the way the world around us functions. Through his art, he searches for “the gap between reality and illusion in the forgotten or ignored margins of the paper, the frayed edge of the image where the picture meets the frame, in the neglected corners of the room, offstage behind the scenery after the performance rather than in the full glare of the theatrical spectacle.”

The art show was a great success with students, parents, teachers, and members of the greater St. John’s community in attendance. Many were very moved by his work and just a few of the many reactions are written below:

Within Mr. Mitchell’s art pieces, one can see the explorations of past artists combining with elements of the reality of our world which we all question. He hopes that his work will allow us to answer a fundamental question of how art relates to human existence in general, “Could painting… be a useful tool for contemplating the ultimate as well as the conditional nature of reality?” If you were at the art show, then hopefully his article furthered your enjoyment of the exhibition and if you have no yet seen it, hopefully you are much inclined to visit the exhibition (open until February 14th) and explore your own reality and perspectives through viewing Mr. Mitchell’s art.
