Green Book Movie Review

By: George Hak

Peter Farrelly’s, “The Green Book,” is an exciting movie about identity and courage. The movie tells the tale of Dr. Don Shirley, a homosexual African American played by Mahershala Ali, and his musical tour of the American South in the 1960s. Dr. Shirley is accompanied by Tony Lip, played by Viggo Mortensen, an Italian mobster who drives Dr. Shirley to the various locations on his tour.


The first positive aspect of the film was the acting. The terrific performance by Mahershala Ali elevates this movie to a film of high-quality. Ali is able to express many different and complex emotions throughout the movie, especially during the scene between him and Viggo Mortensen (his co-star with whom he has great chemistry) in the rain. The second positive aspect of the film is the music and sound. The movie employs many great songs from African-American musicians (Chubby Chace, Little Richard, and Aretha Franklin) in the 1960s (Slow Twistin’, Lucille, and Won’t Be Long). Not only does the music fit the setting, but it also juxtaposes Mahershala Ali’s character and his refusal to play popular music with other prevalent musicians of the 1960s who embraced it.


The first negative aspect of the film is the ending. The ending is quite “Hollywood” and is untrue to certain characters’ natures. The only characters who truly evolve during the film are Viggo Mortensen’s character and Mahershala Ali’s character. Thus, it seems quite unnatural for minor characters in the film to have the same change in personality as Mortensen's character, which ultimately negatively affects the film’s ending. The second negative aspect is the method in which the main message of the story is presented. Although the main message of the story, which I won’t spoil in the review, is poetic and socially relevant, the method used by the storytellers to transmit the message was not carried out well. Like the director Spike Lee pointed out, the idea that it takes a racist white Italian man to educate an African-American man about his own culture is ridiculous.


Ultimately, the Green Book is a very enjoyable film with great performances from Mahershala Ali and Viggo Mortensen, and a great selection of music. Although there are some negative aspects, such as the ending and the method used to transmit the main message of the story, the movie is still very good and I highly recommend watching it.
