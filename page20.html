New Student Orientation

Written By: Aimee Dastin-van Rijn

Middle and High School New Student Orientation took place on Tuesday, August 22nd. While all the students started in an assembly which gave them information about St. John’s and introduced them to some important people, the high schoolers soon split off from the middle schoolers. There were more new students than were expected, with the most coming in to 11th Grade.

All the high schoolers went to C-Level, where they were introduced to many important people that all high school students must know. The students then began to get to know each other by grade level. They asked about names, nationality, interests, among many other topics. As is typical of an international school, everyone was very different, but these differences are what make St. John’s so special.

"As is typical of an international school, everyone was very different, but these differences are what make St. John’s so special."

All the high school students then moved on to play games to learn each other's names or other facts or played ninja and voltage (drama games that almost all high school students know.) After the games, they split into groups to discuss the different aspects of student life and proceeded to tour campus with the student ambassadors as leaders. 


Once all the students (including the middle schoolers) were back on C-Level, Ms. Cooke went through the new student handbook with them, explaining every section in depth so no student would be confused. 


By the end of this, all the students were starving, so they all went to the cafeteria to enjoy a school-provided lunch and continue socializing. 


After orientation was over, this was the reaction of a new 11th grade student, Julia Bastos, "The orientation day at St. John's was great! I felt very welcomed and happy right from the beginning. The staff was very nice and my peers, too. At first I was quite shy, but I felt so comfortable in such a short time and it seemed that I'd been friends with everyone for years. People at St. John's really make you feel like part of the family, it's great!"


Ashley Webster, another new 11th grade student added, "The 2017 Orientation day was a great success! After walking onto campus we were greeted with welcoming smiles from the many parents, staff, and students. The overwhelming idea of starting a new school in a new country seemed daunting at first, but the staff and orientation counselors immediately put me at ease. After this experience, I feel excited and confident about starting school and I am looking forward to being a part of this amazing St. Johns community."
