The Hague International Model United Nations

An Incredible Experience

Every year, thousands of delegates gather at the World Forum in The Hague to participate in The Hague International Model United Nations. THIMUN is a simulation of the United Nations for high school students. This year, at THIMUN’s 50th conference, we had 11 students participating.


Our students composed a delegation representing the country of Uzbekistan, with 9 students being delegates, one International Court of Justice judge, and one MUNITY Online Press member. Over the course of the five days, the students learned more about world issues, public speaking, leadership, and how to become change makers.

MUN is not truly appreciated for the amazing activity that it is, as one cannot know how wonderful it feels to have one’s resolution be passed or how fantastic it feels to ask a point of information in front of a couple hundred of your peers, until you have actually experienced it. The conference seemed to exceed the expectations of every student involved.


An 11th grade 1st-time THIMUN student Maxime said, “THIMUN is an amazing experience as you learn more about the world and about diplomatic relations in the purpose of solving big issues. I learned so much, including how to speak in front of 175 people while using diplomatic language, as well as how to write resolutions.”

Nothing truly bonds a group of people more than a dedication to making the world a better place. From taking the tram to seeing Princess Mabel van Oranje speak on child marriage to all-you-can-eat sushi as a group to the rousing music during the closing ceremony, THIMUN 2018 has influenced us all for the better. The whole group plans to attend again next year, and others are encouraged to join MUN so that they can have this experience in the coming years.
