St. John's Fashion Shoot

We all know that St. John's has many talented artists, but our fashion designers have yet to be discovered. Here is the result of St. John's first
student-designed fashion shoot.

Marie Wuyts

Marie began wishing to pursue fashion at the age of 5 or 6. She used to design clothing for her Barbies, a small step towards the amazing clothes she is now designing. She continued on this path, making bags out of outgrown pairs of pants. Later in childhood, Marie decided she would like to be a neurosurgeon, which lasted for a few years, but, about a year ago, her passion for fashion resurfaced.

Marie’s goal for her fashion career would be to be like Yves Saint Laurent, who started from nothing, was later hired by Dior, and ended up founding his own fashion company. As a little kid, Marie was amazed when she saw her mother’s bags designed by him and visited the museum in Paris featuring his work, further inspiring her. She is pursuing her goal of founding her own fashion company through currently studying IB Business and IB Art. 

Marie’s process is not like every other fashion designer. Just like her young self, Marie’s clothes are made out of other clothing that is no longer being worn. She asks others if they have any clothes they are no longer using and searches vintage clothing stores for cheap clothing which suits what she needs. Once she finds these, she unstitches them and begins to create something new.

Whenever Marie gets an idea, she draws it, but it never turns out how she originally planned. Through the process, she feels how it is going while actually making it and does whatever feels right in the moment. From Barbies to models, Marie had begun her journey towards changing the world of fashion, so, in ten years we should all be searching the racks for the latest Marie Wuyts design.

Manya Anand

Since the age of 5, when she loved poufy dresses and shopping, Manya has wanted to be a fashion designer. She used to walk through the racks of clothing with an amazed appreciation, dreaming of the days when she could create her own designs. As she grew up, she drew many designs, but never actually made them come to life by making them out of real fabric. Here at St. John’s, she and a friend, Helen Sietses, began going to stores and looking online for the type of clothing they wanted to create. They found that spring colors, bright and floral, were the look they wanted to go for.

Manya would design the clothing and then both she and Helen would begin to sew by hand. After that, they would finish off the outfits using Helen’s sewing machines. They made four dresses, two of which were casual and two for going out.

Manya highly encourages more people to participate in the fashion show next year for this amazing experience.
